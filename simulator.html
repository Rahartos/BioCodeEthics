How Does CRISPR Work?

CRISPR/Cas9 is a gene-editing tool that allows scientists to make precise changes to DNA. It uses a guide RNA to target a specific DNA sequence and a Cas9 protein to cut the DNA at the desired location.

Once the DNA is cut, the cell's natural repair mechanisms can be used to add, remove, or replace genetic material.

What is DNA?

DNA is the genetic blueprint of life, composed of four letters:

  • A = Adenine
  • T = Thymine
  • C = Cytosine
  • G = Guanine

Sequences like ATCGGATACGTAGCTAGGATCGT carry genetic information. CRISPR is a gene editing tool that targets a DNA segment using a guide RNA and replaces it with a new sequence.

Example: CRISPR can replace TACGTA in your DNA with GGGCCC.
